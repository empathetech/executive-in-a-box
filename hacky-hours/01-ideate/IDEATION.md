## Raw Ideas

- AI/LLM-powered CEO guidance tool for solo builders and small teams
- "Board of directors" made up of different LLMs that collaborate, debate, and argue — user is the tiebreaker and final decision-maker
- User picks which LLM(s) they want on their board
- Tool helps users build org structures that benefit workers equally — co-ops, employee-owned companies, democratic orgs
- A resource for people who got laid off and are effectively "laying off their CEO" — reclaiming power
- A direct response to executives using AI to eliminate labor thoughtlessly and cruelly
- The user is always the one who adopts any position — the LLMs advise, they don't decide

## Who Would Use This First?

Someone who just got laid off and is starting something of their own. They don't need a CEO — they need CEO-level thinking available on demand. Also anyone who wants to build something more democratic and worker-friendly than the structures they came from. They want the skills without the hierarchy.

## The Problem

The people who need strategic/executive guidance the most — solo builders, co-op founders, workers starting their own thing — are exactly the people who can't afford to hire a CEO or consultant. Meanwhile, the executives making decisions that destroy workers' lives are using AI as the justification. This tool flips that: put those same capabilities in workers' hands.

## Use Cases & Feature Ideas

### For new orgs
- Helps solo builders or new teams set up democratic org structures (co-ops, employee-owned companies)
- Provides strategic/executive guidance without needing to hire or defer to a human CEO

### For existing orgs
- Audits a current company structure and builds a structured, comprehensive case for replacing the executive team with this tool
- Gives workers ammunition to make the case internally for a more democratic structure

### CEO-replacement capabilities
- Manages LinkedIn presence — networking with other executives, finding/securing funding sources
- Pitches the product to investors, partners, and people who can help the company succeed
- Plans, hosts, and conducts all-hands meetings (direction, focus, new features, etc.)
- Acts as the face/voice of the org — a steward of the org's principles as defined by the people in it
- Stays current on AI integrations and communicates their impact and value clearly
- Handles stakeholder KPIs/ROIs/metrics in a non-jargony, actually-meaningful way
- Sheds the bad and ineffective parts of the CEO role while keeping what makes a CEO genuinely useful

### The board of directors model
- Multiple LLMs (user's choice) act as board members — they debate and argue
- User is always the tiebreaker and final decision-maker; the tool advises, never decides
- The tool represents the voice/mission/values that the org itself developed

### Easter egg: The Revenge Feature (low priority, high delight)
- Inspired by the romance movie trope: the hero gets dumped, finds something better
- User can use the tool to ideate what a better version of their old product would have looked like if they and their colleagues hadn't been laid off for bad reasons
- Not a primary feature — a hidden, joyful, cathartic one
- "What would we have built if they hadn't made a stupid decision?"

## What Does Success Look Like?

A user posts publicly: "It turns out we needed a CEO that was actually plugged into the product and the people. The value of a human CEO is overblown 90% of the time and this is a tool that gets us what we actually need in terms of value from a CEO without the cost and baggage of having to have an actual CEO slow us down and hurt people and the product in order to please shareholders and extract money at the expense of everyone and everything else."

Core insight: the human CEO's value is real but overblown. The cost — financial, human, political — is not. This tool captures the value, cuts the cost, and puts the people who do the actual work back in control.
